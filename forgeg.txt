### Issue kya hai? (Dry Run)
Tumhare ArchUnit ka rule keh raha hai:
1. `Repository` ko **SIRF** `Service` touch karegi.
2. `Service` ko **SIRF** `Controller` touch karega.

**Lekin Spring Boot ki reality alag hoti hai:**
* **Schedulers (`..scheduler..`)**: Inko background jobs chalane ke liye `Service` ko call karna hi padta hai.
* **Configs & Initializers (`..config..` / `ForgePlatformApplication`)**: Application start hote hi default data (Admin user, categories) daalne ke liye inhe `Repository` ya `Service` ki zaroorat padti hai.
* **Security (`ApplicationConfig`)**: Authentication ke liye `UserDetailsService` banate waqt `UserRepository` chahiye hota hai.

Tumhara ArchUnit rule ekdum rigid watchman ban gaya hai jo inn "Maintenance Staff" (Schedulers/Configs) ko building mein ghusne hi nahi de raha.

### The Enterprise Solution (2 Options):
Barclays ya kisi bhi top Pune firm mein, hum isko aise handle karte hain:

**Option 1 (Highly Recommended): Update the ArchUnit Rule.**
Hum `ArchitectureTest.java` mein rule ko "Spring Boot Aware" banate hain. Hum declare karte hain ki `Scheduler` layer bhi `Service` ko access kar sakti hai, aur `Config` layer bhi zaroorat padne par setup kar sakti hai.

**Option 2 (Code Refactoring): Move everything to Services.**
Hum Configs aur `ForgePlatformApplication` (main class) se saare `save()` methods nikal kar ek `DatabaseSeederService` banayenge. Schedulers ko satisfy karne ke liye rule toh thoda change karna hi padega.
